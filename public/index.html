Customer Segmentation

Not every customer needs the same level of attention. Segment early, even if you only have one CSM.

SMB

0–50 employees

Lean teams with fast decisions, typically owned by 1–2 stakeholders. Success depends on ease of use, quick time-to-value, and minimal friction to get up and running.

Mid-Market

51–500 employees

Scaling organizations with increasing complexity. Requires alignment between a champion and an executive sponsor. Success depends on product adoption and relationship management.

Enterprise

500+ employees

Complex organizations with multiple stakeholders and formal processes. Success requires multi-threaded relationships, cross-team adoption, and structured engagement from the outset.

Key Takeaway

GRR tells you if you're bleeding. NRR tells you if your existing customers believe in you enough to give you more money. If NRR is above 100%, you can grow revenue even with zero new sales. That's the goal.

Scaling Up

Process Without the Bureaucracy

The teams that scale well don't choose between structure and speed — they build just enough of the right process and protect CSM autonomy everywhere else.

Coverage Model in Practice

The same three segments you defined in the Foundation — here's exactly how each one operates at scale.

Onboarding

Automated email sequence on days 1, 3, 7, 14, 30. In-app checklist. Monthly group webinar for all new SMB customers. No 1:1 kickoff unless they request it.

Ongoing Engagement

Monthly group office hours. Automated health score alerts. Triggered check-ins when score drops below threshold. Product updates via email and in-app banners.

Renewal Motion

60 days out: automated renewal email. 30 days out: CSM reviews health score and reaches out if at-risk. Self-serve renewal via billing portal.

Onboarding

Dedicated kickoff call within 48h of signing. Shared project plan in Notion or Google Doc. Weekly check-ins for first 60 days. Introduction to champion and exec sponsor in week one.

Ongoing Engagement

Quarterly business reviews. Assigned CSM reachable via Slack or email. Monthly usage digest auto-sent to champion. Product feedback routed through CSM to product team.

Renewal Motion

90 days out: CSM prepares renewal deck with ROI evidence. 60 days: formal renewal conversation. 30 days: procurement involvement. Target NRR improvement via upsell discussion.

Onboarding

Custom success plan signed before go-live. Dedicated onboarding project with milestones. Weekly calls. Exec sponsor alignment in first two weeks. Integration support included.

Ongoing Engagement

Weekly tactical calls with champion. Monthly exec check-ins. Semi-annual EBRs with C-suite. Dedicated Slack channel. Proactive roadmap sharing 90 days in advance.

Renewal Motion

120 days out: internal red/green review. 90 days: EBR tied to renewal conversation. 60 days: multi-threaded outreach. 30 days: legal and procurement loop. Expansion tied to renewal close.

Operating Rhythm

Every cadence below has a defined owner and output.

Renewal Pipeline Review

Weekly

Review all renewals within 90 days. Surfaces risk early and drives account-level action before deadlines approach.

Output:
Action plan

At-Risk Account Deep Dive

Bi-Weekly

CSM identifies root cause and puts a success plan in place. Ensures no at-risk account sits without a response.

Output:
Success plan

Process Retrospective

Monthly

Address process debt before it compounds. Ask 'what slowed us down this month?' and commit to one fix.

Output:
Process change

Coverage Model Review

Quarterly

Review account sizes and complexity to ensure each account is in the right tier and receiving the right level of attention.

Output:
Tier assignments

Key Takeaway

The goal isn't a perfect process — it's a process good enough that your best CSMs don't need it, and your newest CSMs can rely on it. Document the 20% of situations that cause 80% of the confusion. Leave the rest to judgment.
